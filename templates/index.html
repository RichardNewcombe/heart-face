Detect your heart rate from your face using Eulerian Video Magnification — the same algorithm from MIT CSAIL that makes the pulse in your skin visible to the naked eye.

How HeartFace Works

HeartFace implements the core ideas of Eulerian Video Magnification (Wu et al., MIT 2012) for contact-free heart rate measurement (rPPG).

  1. Gaussian pyramid — each video frame is spatially decomposed into a multi-scale pyramid. The coarsest level (a single, spatially-averaged pixel per colour channel) captures the broadest colour variation across the face.
  2. Temporal signal — across hundreds of frames we record how the mean R, G, B colour of your forehead changes over time. Blood pumping through skin capillaries causes a periodic colour shift of ≈0.5%.
  3. CHROM normalisation — illumination changes are cancelled by computing the chrominance ratio 3R − 2G, isolating haemoglobin absorption.
  4. Bandpass + FFT — the signal is filtered to 0.75–4 Hz (45–240 BPM) and a Fast Fourier Transform finds the dominant frequency → your heart rate.
  5. EVM visualisation — pressing "Show Pulse Glow" amplifies the bandpassed signal as a colour overlay on your face, making the pulse visible just like the original MIT paper demos.

All processing runs entirely in your browser. No video is ever sent to a server.
